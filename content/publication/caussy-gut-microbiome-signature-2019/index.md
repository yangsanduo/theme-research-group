abstract: The presence of cirrhosis in nonalcoholic-fatty-liver-disease (NAFLD) is
  the most important predictor of liver-related mortality. Limited data exist concerning
  the diagnostic accuracy of gut-microbiome-derived signatures for detecting NAFLD-cirrhosis.
  Here we report 16S gut-microbiome compositions of 203 uniquely well-characterized
  participants from a prospective twin and family cohort, including 98 probands encompassing
  the entire spectrum of NAFLD and 105 of their first-degree relatives, assessed by
  advanced magnetic-resonance-imaging. We show strong familial correlation of gut-microbiome
  profiles, driven by shared housing. We report a panel of 30 features, including
  27 bacterial features with discriminatory ability to detect NAFLD-cirrhosis using
  a Random Forest classifier model. In a derivation cohort of probands, the model
  has a robust diagnostic accuracy (AUROC of 0.92) for detecting NAFLD-cirrhosis,
  confirmed in a validation cohort of relatives of proband with NAFLD-cirrhosis (AUROC
  of 0.87). This study provides evidence for a fecal-microbiome-derived signature
  to detect NAFLD-cirrhosis.
